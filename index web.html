Welcome to Bandung

Bandung is the capital city of the West Java province of Indonesia. Located on the island of Java, Greater Bandung (Bandung Basin Metropolitan Area / BBMA) is third-most populous city in Indonesia after Jakarta and Surabaya and the country's second-largest and second most populous metropolitan area, with over 11 million inhabitants.

Situated 768 meters (2,520 feet) above sea level, approximately 140 kilometres (87 miles) southeast of Jakarta, Bandung has cooler year-round temperatures than most other Indonesian cities. The city lies in a river basin surrounded by volcanic mountains that provide a natural defense system.

Quick Facts about Bandung

  • Population: Over 2.5 million people (2023)
  • Elevation: 768 meters above sea level
  • Distance from Jakarta: 140 kilometres southeast
  • Nickname: "Parijs van Java" (The Paris of Java)
  • Province: West Java, Indonesia
  • Climate: Tropical monsoon climate with cooler temperatures

Why Visit Bandung?

Bandung is known for several attractions:

  1. Fashion and Shopping: Famous for textile factories, fashion outlets, and "distro" culture
  2. Cool Climate: Cooler temperatures due to high elevation
  3. Architecture: Dutch colonial architecture and Art Deco buildings
  4. Education Hub: Home to prestigious universities like ITB (Bandung Institute of Technology)
  5. Creative Industry: UNESCO Creative City of Design since 2015
  6. Technology Center: Indonesia's major technology centre

Historical Significance

Bandung played a crucial role in world history when it hosted the first Asian-African Conference (also known as the Bandung Conference) in 1955. This historic event was attended by heads of states representing twenty-nine independent countries from Asia and Africa, promoting world peace and opposition against colonialism.

Economy and Industry

The city's economy is mainly built upon:

  • Tourism and hospitality
  • Creative industries and fashion
  • High-tech and manufacturing industries
  • Educational institutions
  • Technology and innovation
  • Retail services and shopping

Popular shopping destinations include Jalan Cihampelas (known as "jeans street"), Jalan Braga, and various factory outlets throughout the city.

Tourist Attractions Near Bandung

Significant tourist sites include:

  • Tangkuban Prahu: Volcano crater to the north
  • Kawah Putih: Beautiful volcanic lake
  • Tea Plantations: Surrounding the Parahyangan Mountains
  • Museums: Geological Museum, Asian-African Conference Museum
  • Cultural Sites: Gedung Sate, Gedung Merdeka
